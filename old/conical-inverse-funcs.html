Parabolas & Inverses

To find an inverse, we reflect across the bisector line y = x. The full parabola y = x² is not injective; it fails the horizontal line test.

The Solution

1. Restrict Domain: We only consider x ≥ 0.

2. Bijectivity: Now the function is 1-to-1 and onto its range.

3. Inverse: Solve x = y² for y to get y = √x.

y = x² (Domain Restricted)
y = √x (Inverse)
y = x (Symmetry)
I

Injective (1-to-1)

"Ogni elemento del codominio è immagine di al massimo un elemento del dominio."

Fails if two different inputs produce the same output (like a full parabola).

S

Surjective (Onto)

"L'immagine della funzione coincide con il codominio."

Every single point in the target set is "hit" by the function.

B

Bijective

"Invertibile."

Both injective and surjective. Required for a unique inverse function to exist.
